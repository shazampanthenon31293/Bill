Definitions.- In this title-
    (1) DESIGNATED BILLS.-The term 'Designated Bills' means legislation chosen by either the chair or ranking member of either the Senate Budget Committee or the House Budget Committee.
    (2) DESIGNATING RESOLUTION.- The term 'Designating Resolution' means any legislation to designate a bill for the prupouses of this title.
    (3) BANNED PROVISISONS.- The term 'Banned Provisions' or 'provison is banned' means a provison that does anything following-
     (A) Lessen the amount of time to vote;
     (B) Gets rid of early voting periods;
     (C) Gets rid of Vote-In Mail;
     (D) Redistricts for the undue benfit of either a party, individual or rac
     (E) Prohibits same day registration;
     (F) Empowers partisan poll watchers;
     (G) Gives more election power to state legislators;
     (H) Makes it eaiser to Cage voters;
     (I) Prohibiting automatic voter registration; and
     (J) Devolves election power to the states unless it is only to provide more democracy;
     (K) Does not affect voting;
     (L) Violates the constitution;
     (M) Eliminated independent redistricting commissions;
     (N) Restricts voting access;
     (O) Restricts voting registration;
     (P) Denies the right of ex-felons to vote;
     (Q) Allowing States to restricting curbside voting;
     (R) Allowing States to restrictions on donations of food and beverages at polling stations;
     (S) Allowing States to close polling places to curb turnout from certian groups;
     (T) Banning the use of sworn written statement to meet identification requirements for voting; 
     (U) Allowing release of personal information;
     (V) Allowing mid-decade redistricting; or
     (W) Provisons not relating to voting
    (4) JUSTIFIED PROVISONS.- The term "Justified Provisons" or "Provisons that are Justified" means an provison that is not banned under paragraph (3)
    (5) AMENDMENT IN NATURE OF A SUBSTITUTE.- the term "Amendment in Nature of a Substitute" means any amendment that proposes to replace the entire text of the measure.
    (6) VOTER CAGING.- The term "voter caging" or "cage voters" means any attempt challenging the registration status of voters

    (8) ORIGINAL MEASURE.- The term "Original Measure" means an introduced bill that embodies a text approved in a committee markup but not formally introduced prior to the markup
    (9) APPROPRIATE CONGRESSIONAL COMMITTEES.- The term "Appropriate Congressional Committees" means The Senate Committee on Rules and Administration or the House Committee on House Administration

Sec._________________
(a) Designation of A Bill.- The concurrent resolution to designate a bill and the bill that will be expidited must be a Original Measure. After the introduction of the bill it must be signed for by the Chairman of one of the appropriate congressional committees (It must be the committee that marked up the bill). 

(b) Debate in the Senate.-Debate in the Senate on any designate bill or resolution (can either be simple or concurrent resolutions) to designate bills reported by the Senate or House Budget Committee, and all amendments there to and debatable motions and appeals in connection therewith, shall be limited to not more than 20 hours. The debate time shall not count time in which the senate is having a roll call vote. The bill and resolution must be sponsored by the same Senator or Repersentive. The sponsor must be the chair or ranking member of either the Senate Committee on Rules and Administration or the House Committee on House Administration. Time for debate must be split equally between the majority and minority parties. 

(c) Prohibiting Amendments.-Amendments shall not be offered on the resolution to designate bills. 

(d) After Debate Expiring.-Immediately after the fourty hours have expired, the senate shall take a roll call vote on wether to pass the bill. The bill shall only be required to pass by a simple majority (not withstanding any provison of the constitution). If the bill passes then the Secretary shall send a note to the clerk of the house . If the bill has passed in a identical form in the house then the bill shall be enrolled with in seven days. If they have not passed it then the House shall take up the bill within 72 hours of getting the message. If not then any repersentive may require for the chamber to come back into session with the signatures of themselves and ten other members. The clerk of the house or the secretary of the senate shall make sure that the signatures are authentic. If he/she determines that the signatures are authentic then she shall sign a document requiring the reconveing of the chamber within 12 hours. 

(e) Point of Order against Adjournment.-It shall not be in order in either the House of Representatives or the Senate to consider any resolution or motion to Adjourn either designate bill or resolution has been voted up or down in both chambers. This may only be waived by unamious consent. The member who asks for adjournment must give the chamber a reason in the form of a written statement that shall be entered into the congressional record.


(f) One Amendement.- The senate majority leader or his/her designee may offer only one Amendment in Nature of a Substitute during the entire debate over the bill. The senate minority leader or his/her designee may offer only one Amendment in Nature of a Substitute during the entire debate over the bill. Once a Amendment in Nature of a Substitute is offered it must follow this procedure-
    (1) Debate for 5 hours;
    (2) Equall split between the majority and minority;
    (3) The chair and ranking member of the Senate Committee on Rules and Administration;
    (4) No amendement to the Amendment in Nature of a Substitute may be offered (except if allowed by unamious consent);
    (5) After debate has expired then the amendement must be voted up or down; 
    (6) None of this debate time for Amendment in Nature of a Substitute shall not count towards the 40 hours described in subsection (a)


(g) Striken Provisons.-A provison may be striken from this bill if the provison is banned. It shall just affect the banned provisons and nothing else. A provison may not be banned if it is necessary to make a jusified provison work. The parlimentarian shall make note in the congressional record. This note shall include the following-
    (1) The sponsor;
    (2) Any cosponsors;
    (3) Text of striken provision;
    (4) Why it was striken;

Sec.________ Slip Form Publication
In publishing this Act in slip form and in the United States Statutes at Large pursuant to section 112 of title 1, United States Code, the Archivist of the United States shall include after the date of approval an appendix setting forth the text of each bill, resolution, and other provision referred to in the previous section. This law shall also be published in slip form and made avaliable online within six months of enactement. The Archivist of the United States is authorized to temporaily hire more staff to comply with the provisions of this section.
