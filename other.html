Sec _________ Point of Order

(a) Point Of Order.—

(1) IN GENERAL.—In the Senate, it shall not be in order to consider a provision in a bill, joint resolution, motion, amendment, amendment between the Houses, or conference report that contains convered provisons. It shall follow the procedure laid out in subsection (b)

(2) POINT OF ORDER SUSTAINED.—If a point of order is made by a Senator against a provision described in paragraph (1), and the point of order is sustained by the Chair, that provision shall be stricken from the measure and may not be offered as an amendment from the floor.

(b) Procedure for Point of Motion.- The senator shall rise and say “Mr. President, I rise to point of order”. The president shall ask the senator to explain. The senator then shall say "This violates Section _____ of Modern America Act" after this then it shall follow the procedure laid out in this rest of this section

(c) Conference Reports.—When the Senate is considering a conference report on, or an amendment between the Houses in relation to, a bill or joint resolution, upon a point of order being made by any Senator pursuant to subsection (a)(1), and such point of order being sustained, such material contained in such conference report or House amendment shall be stricken, and the Senate shall proceed to consider the question of whether the Senate shall recede from its amendment and concur with a further amendment, or concur in the House amendment with a further amendment, as the case may be, which further amendment shall consist of only that portion of the conference report or House amendment, as the case may be, not so stricken. Any such motion in the Senate shall be debatable. In any case in which such point of order is sustained against a conference report (or Senate amendment derived from such conference report by operation of this subsection), no further amendment shall be in order.

(d) Supermajority Waiver And Appeal.—In the Senate, this section may be waived or suspended only by an affirmative vote of three-fourths of the Members, duly chose and sworn. An affirmative vote of two-thirds of Members of the Senate, duly chosen and sworn shall be required to sustain an appeal of the ruling of the Chair on a point of order raised under this section.

Sec _________ Procedure

(a) Designation of A Bill.- The concurrent resolution to designate a bill and the bill that will be expidited must be a Original Measure. After the introduction of the bill it must be signed for by the Chairman of one of the appropriate congressional committees (It must be the committee that marked up the bill).

(b) Debate in the Senate.-Debate in the Senate on any designate bill or resolution (can either be simple or concurrent resolutions) to designate bills reported by the Senate or House Budget Committee, and all amendments there to and debatable motions and appeals in connection therewith, shall be limited to not more than 20 hours. The debate time shall not count time in which the senate is having a roll call vote. The bill and resolution must be sponsored by the same Senator or Repersentive. The sponsor must be the chair or ranking member of either the Senate Committee on Rules and Administration or the House Committee on House Administration. Time for debate must be split equally between the majority and minority parties. During the consideration of the bill or the concurrent resolution to designate a bill the Vice President of the Senate (As president of the senate) shall proside over the debate of the bill. Voting on amendements shall not count towards the 20 hours of debate.

(c) Prohibiting Amendments.-Amendments shall not be offered on the resolution to designate bills. Any attempt to offer amendement shall be considered not in order. This point of order shall only be waived by a unamious consent agreement (with a quoroum present when the unamious consent agreement is offered)

(d) After Debate Expiring.-Immediately after the fourty hours have expired, the senate shall take a roll call vote on wether to pass the bill. The bill shall only be required to pass by a simple majority (not withstanding any provison of the constitution). If the bill passes then the Secretary shall send a note to the clerk of the house . If the bill has passed in a identical form in the house then the bill shall be enrolled with in seven days. If they have not passed it then the House shall take up the bill within 72 hours of getting the message. If not then any repersentive may require for the chamber to come back into session with the signatures of themselves and ten other members. The clerk of the house or the secretary of the senate shall make sure that the signatures are authentic. If he/she determines that the signatures are authentic then she shall sign a document requiring the reconveing of the chamber within 12 hours. The clerk of the house or the secretary of the senate shall notify the other about the reconveing of the chamber.

(e) Point of Order against Adjournment.-It shall not be in order in either the House of Representatives or the Senate to consider any resolution or motion to Adjourn either designate bill or resolution has been voted up or down in both chambers. This may only be waived by unamious consent. The member who asks for adjournment must give the chamber a reason in the form of a written statement that shall be entered into the congressional record.

(f) One Amendement.- The senate majority or minority leader or his/her designee may offer only one Amendment in Nature of a Substitute during the entire debate over the bill. The senate minority leader or his/her designee may offer only one Amendment in Nature of a Substitute during the entire debate over the bill. Once a Amendment in Nature of a Substitute is offered it must follow this procedure-

(1) Debate for 7 hours;

(2) Equally split between the majority and minority;

(3) The vice president shall preside over the Senate during the consideration of the Amendment in Nature of a Substitute

(4) The chair and ranking member of the Senate Committee on Rules and Administration (Or their designees) shall get priority recognition;

(5) No amendment to the Amendment in Nature of a Substitute may be offered during the debate on Amendment in Nature of a Substitute (except if allowed by unanimous consent);

(6) After debate has expired then the amendment must be voted up or down;

(7) None of this debate time for Amendment in Nature of a Substitute shall count towards the 40 hours described in subsection (a)

(8) The Majority and Minority leader each shall speak at least once during the debate unless it is waived by unanimous consent agreement (With at least a quorum in the Senate at the time)
